Cryptocurrencies - Digital Assets

Cryptocurrencies are decentralized digital assets that use blockchain technology to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. Bitcoin, launched in 2009, is the first and most well-known cryptocurrency, followed by Ethereum, Ripple, Litecoin, and thousands of others.

Cryptocurrencies offer a secure and efficient way of transferring funds without the need for intermediaries like banks. They are used for investments, payments, and even as a hedge against inflation in some cases.
